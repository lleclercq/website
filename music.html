Nick's music is the result of a wide range of influence. Most notable is the classic rock from the 1960s and 1970s. Artists such as Jimi Hendrix, Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, and the Beatles formed the roots of Nick's early guitar playing. Later on, Nick discovered the Red Hot Chili Peppers. Guitarist John Frusciante's perfect combination of funk and rock on the 1991 album Blood Sugar Sex Magik had a profound impact.

Nick started looking into funk music. George Clinton's Parliament and Funkadelic (P-Funk) were obvious listening choices. If you follow that music back even further into the 1960s, you arrive at the iconic James Brown. Nick explored James Brown's musical family tree and found other jazz, funk, and soul artists such as Herbie Hancock, Sly and the Family Stone, The Meters, and more.

Nick's roots can ultimately be traced back to James Brown and Jimi Hendrix. He's most happy with a tight, funky drummer, and a groovy bassline. His guitar style is in the same vein as Hendrix, agressive and bluesy with a touch of funk. We hope you enjoy.
